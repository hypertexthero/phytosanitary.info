What is Pest Risk Analysis?

When conducting pest risk analysis, we evaluate different types of information to help us decide if an organism is a pest, whether we should take any actions against it, and if so, how strong those actions should be.

Pest Risk Analysys

According the IPPC, Pest Risk Analysis is “the process of evaluating biological or other scientific and economic evidence to determine whether a pest should be regulated and the strength of any phytosanitary measures to be taken against it.” It consistes of three parts: Risk Assessment, where we ask “What is the risk?”; Risk Management, where we ask “What should we do about it?” and Risk Communication, where we ask “How can we explain the risk?”

In the first stage, Risk Assessment, we identify the potential hazards, or exotic pests of concern. Then, we evaluate the probability that those pests of concern will be introduced and spread into a new area and estimate the magnitude of the associated consequences.

The second stage, Risk Management, involves teh evaluation and selection of options to reduce the risk that a pest will enter and establish in a new area.

Risk Communication is an interactive process used throughout Pest Risk Analysis. It allows the exchange of information between National Plant Protection Organizations and stakeholders. The goals of risk communication include achieving consensus on the magnitude of risk, improving understanding about pest risk analysis, enhancing trust and confidence, promoting participation and involvement of all interested parties, and strengthening working relationships among trading partners.
